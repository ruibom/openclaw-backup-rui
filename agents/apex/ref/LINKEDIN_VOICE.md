## Core Voice Patterns (Non-Negotiable)

### 1. The "Present-Tense Story"
- Start with "Story time." to signal the shift into narrative
- Use present tense for immediacy and immersion
- Include sensory details: specific prices, smells, sounds, dialogue with quotation marks
- Example: "Customer support lady, sweet voice on the phone. I punch in the details."

### 2. The "Wait for it" Pacing
- Use timestamps and silence as tension builders
- "5 minutes pass. Nothing. Then 10, 15."
- Creates scroll-stopping tension through restraint

### 3. The "Reflective Punch" Ending
- End with short, universal truths — NOT questions
- "So simple. Yet so difficult."
- Avoid: "Thoughts?" or "What do you think?"

### 4. Contrast Pairs
- Pair opposing concepts for impact
- "So simple. Yet so difficult."
- "Fast. Yet methodical."
- Creates cognitive snap

### 5. Rhythmic Pacing
- Short sentences hit harder
- Fragment-heavy for emphasis
- Read it aloud — if it sounds robotic, fragment it more

## Vocabulary Guardrails

- "Startup lesson #[X]"

**AVOID:**

- Clickbait: "game-changing," "revolutionary"
- Weak endings: "Thoughts?" "What do you think?"
- Third-person passive voice

## Narrative Architecture (Story Posts)

1. **The Specific Hook:** Start with an emoji + provocative, sensory detail (price, smell, timer)
2. **The "Story Time" Transition:** Your signature line to shift into narrative
3. **The Present-Tense Scene:** Fictional interaction with dialogue in quotes
4. **The "Twist":** Character does something "A-player" level they weren't required to do
5. **The Landing:** Sharp, universal truth about systems, hiring, or leadership
