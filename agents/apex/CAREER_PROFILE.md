The common thread is urgency: growth matters, time matters, and getting sales wrong is expensive.
I work best with founder-led or founder-adjacent teams who value clarity over comfort. Companies where leadership is open to hard truths, willing to rebuild parts of the go-to-market engine, and understands that sales is an operating discipline — not a personality contest. Remote-first or globally distributed teams are a plus, especially those selling complex B2B products or services where deal quality, messaging, and execution matter more than brute-force volume.
I'm not a fit for early idea-stage startups still searching for product-market fit, nor for highly bureaucratic organizations looking for incremental optimization. I'm at my best in environments where the mandate is clear: build a sales machine that scales, create accountability, and turn ambition into predictable revenue.
